Every solo album Beyonce has debuted launched at number one on the Billboard charts.

  1. Crazy in Love was her first album, produced and collaborated on with now-husband Jay-Z. Hit songs from this album include “Crazy in Love” and “Baby Boy.”
  2. No one is perfect. B’Day is all of Beyonce’s lamest works on one album, but at least “Irreplaceable” is still car-jamming worthy.
  3. Don’t act like you still don’t know all the words to “Single Ladies” from I Am . . . Sasha Fierce.
  4. 4 is the best slumber party album ever: boys-not-allowed, groovy pop and reggae combinations in songs feature classics like “Party” and “Run the World (Girls)”.
  5. While 4 is a fun album, I still can’t get over the amazing turnaround of quality, theme, and musical innovation that is this album. Beyonce made her the ultimate artist she is, and me a better feminist.
